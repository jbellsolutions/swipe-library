EUGENE SCHWARTZ'S 3-HOUR WRITING SYSTEM

How to Kill Writer's Block and Become a Master Copywriter in Only 3 Hours a Day

Legendary copywriter Eugene Schwartz developed a remarkably simple approach to productive writing. He worked only 3 hours daily, 5 days weekly, yet produced 9 books, dozens of successful advertisements, and countless articles.

== THE METHOD ==

His technique centered on a 33.33-minute timer set at his writing desk. The rules were strict:

- He could drink coffee or stare out the window
- He could sit doing nothing
- He could write
- He could NOT leave his chair
- He could NOT do anything else

"Boredom was the key." Schwartz would sit until tedium forced him to write. Once the timer sounded, he'd stop -- even mid-sentence -- take a 10-15 minute break, then repeat six times daily.

== SUCCESS THROUGH PREPARATION ==

Before writing, Schwartz invested heavily in research and craft development. He'd read and reread product information, systematically documenting benefits. This groundwork meant he arrived at his desk fully prepared, needing only disciplined execution.

== THE PHILOSOPHY ==

Schwartz's success stemmed from mastering fundamentals before applying his timer system. His approach proved that professional achievement doesn't require elaborate strategies -- just consistent, focused effort within defined boundaries.

== KEY SCHWARTZ QUOTES ==

"Copy is not written. Copy is assembled. You do not write copy, you assemble it. You are working with a series of building blocks, you are putting the building blocks together, and then you are putting them in certain structures, you are building a little city of desire for your person to come and live in."

"The greatest mistake marketers make is trying to create demand."

His "hit ratio" of 85% (percentage of winning ads) is one of the highest in copywriting history.
