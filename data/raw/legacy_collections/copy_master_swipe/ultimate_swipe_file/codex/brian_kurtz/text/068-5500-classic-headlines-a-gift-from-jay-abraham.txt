5,500 classic headlines…a gift from Jay Abraham

5 Comments

by Brian Kurtz

October 27, 2016

 Share0

 Tweet0

 Pin0

A few weeks ago I told you how,  “Your mentors choose you”… but I didn’t expect that one of MY mentors, the great Jay Abraham, would choose ALL OF YOU based on my recommendation that you are all awesome subscribers…and students!

Jay recently came to one of my Titans Mastermind meetings and problem-solved for over 2 hours…it was epic. And so valuable to everyone.

Around the same time, Jay and I were both asked to be part of an online “Copywriting Summit” hosted by the amazing Ray Edwards.

Jay did TWO interviews for the Summit: One to “open” for an all star list of speakers and one to close the event as well. 

According to Ray, the interviews with Jay were the two most listened to and watched.

We will send you those interviews for your exclusive viewing NEXT week…they are part of a product with VIP access only at the present time…and I am grateful to both Jay and Ray that I can make those priceless interviews available to you. 

So please watch for those next week.

But THIS week, Jay wanted you to have some of the most valuable “swipes” you will ever download (and hopefully use/ and adapt)…as I mentioned above, Jay is well aware that you are all eager students.

So please add these to your reference library:

5500 of the best headlines ever.

This is such an incredible gift to you from Jay… I have already sent some ideas to some of my clients on how we can adapt some of these headlines for their businesses.

To quote one of the legends profiled in my new book, David Ogilvy:

“On the average, five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy. When you have written your headline, you have spent eighty cents of your dollar.”

I am so happy to share this resource with you. You can’t go wrong following the advice of David Ogilvy…

Consider today’s post content from YOUR mentor Jay Abraham, once removed.

As any world class talent and mentor tends to do, he simply wanted to share incredible wisdom with you…like he has shared with me since the early 1980’s.

Another lesson on mentoring…

Warmly,

Brian

 

P.S. Remember…this is “Part One” (of two) from Jay…watch for the priceless interviews with Jay and Ray Edwards next week. 

And let me know what you think of the 5500 Headlines and some ways you might use them…we can share some of those applications with your fellow subscribers/students.

And remember…a headline can also be a subject line…but you knew that already…
