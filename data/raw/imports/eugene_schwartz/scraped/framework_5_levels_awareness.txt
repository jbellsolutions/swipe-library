EUGENE SCHWARTZ'S 5 LEVELS OF MARKET AWARENESS
From Breakthrough Advertising (1966)

Schwartz's core insight: "In its natural development, every market's awareness passes through several stages. The more aware your market, the easier the selling job, the less you need to say."

== LEVEL 1: UNAWARE (60% of prospects) ==
Consumers don't recognize they have a problem or need. They're unaware the product exists or could help them.

STRATEGY: Create demand by highlighting the problem using evocative imagery and emotional messaging rather than technical details. Use stories, curiosity, and identification.

== LEVEL 2: PROBLEM AWARE (20%) ==
Consumers recognize their need but don't know solutions exist.

STRATEGY: Use empathetic language that acknowledges their struggle while hinting at possibilities. Schwartz emphasized: "No sentence can be effective if it contains only facts. It must also contain emotion, image, logic and promise."

== LEVEL 3: SOLUTION AWARE (10%) ==
Consumers know solutions exist but need convincing yours is best.

STRATEGY: Leverage testimonials, reviews, and case studies demonstrating superiority over alternatives. Show your mechanism -- HOW your solution works.

== LEVEL 4: PRODUCT AWARE (7%) ==
Consumers know your product but hesitate to commit, often due to price.

STRATEGY: Emphasize discounts, offers, and quality-to-price ratio advantages. Overcome specific objections.

== LEVEL 5: MOST AWARE (3%) ==
Consumers are ready to purchase -- they just need transaction details.

STRATEGY: Present compelling offers with clear pricing and purchase instructions. Simple, direct call to action.

== APPLICATION RULES ==

1. Identify which awareness level your prospect is at BEFORE writing copy
2. Match your headline type to the awareness level
3. Never skip levels -- you must meet the prospect where they are
4. The headline's job changes based on awareness level:
   - Unaware: Identify with the reader's situation
   - Problem Aware: Crystallize their problem
   - Solution Aware: Show your unique mechanism
   - Product Aware: Make an irresistible offer
   - Most Aware: State the deal directly
