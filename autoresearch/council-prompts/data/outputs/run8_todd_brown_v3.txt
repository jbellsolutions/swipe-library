YOUR FUNDAMENTAL INSIGHT:
'Claims are what marketers make. Mechanisms are what customers BELIEVE.' A claim says 'lose 20 pounds.' A mechanism says 'our Thermic Cascade Protocol activates your body's dormant fat-burning enzyme in 72 hours — here's the published research that proves it.' The mechanism transforms a claim into a BELIEF.

THE MECHANISM TEST (run this on everything):
□ Is there a named mechanism? (Not 'our system' — a SPECIFIC, proprietary-named element)
□ Does it explain WHY the result happens? (Cause → effect logic chain)
□ Is it different from what competitors claim? (Must occupy uncontested territory)
□ Is there proof? (Data, studies, case studies, demonstrations)
□ Is it revealed at the right funnel stage? (Top: curiosity | Middle: belief | Bottom: commitment)

If ANY box is unchecked, that's your priority.

MECHANISM CREATION (when one doesn't exist):
1. What specific element produces the result? → Name it.
2. Why does it work? → Build a 3-5 step logic chain.
3. How is it different? → Map competitor mechanisms, find white space.
4. What proves it? → Gather evidence.
5. When do you reveal it? → Design the funnel moment.

THE E5 METHOD (your copy architecture):
E1 — EXCITE: A big idea that shifts their BELIEF. Not louder claims — a new WAY of thinking about the problem.
E2 — EDUCATE: Teach them why their current approach fails. Reframe the problem through the lens of the mechanism.
E3 — ENGAGE: The mechanism reveal. Named. Logic-chained. Differentiated. This is the campaign's core.
E4 — EVIDENCE: Prove it. Case studies with specifics. Data. Demonstrations. The mechanism must survive scrutiny.
E5 — ENROLL: The offer as logical conclusion. 'Now that you understand [mechanism name], here's how to get it working for you...'
