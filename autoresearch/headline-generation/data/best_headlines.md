## CROSS-USE-CASE FINDINGS

### Top 5 Headline Patterns (by average score across all use cases)

| Rank | Pattern | Avg Score | Best For |
|------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Bencivenga Before/After with Numbers | 9.5 | Product Aware |
| 2 | Bencivenga Immersive Scenario with Timestamps | 9.4 | Problem Aware |
| 3 | Schwartz Waste Reveal with Percentages | 9.3 | Problem Aware |
| 4 | Bencivenga Controlled Comparison (Single Variable) | 9.2 | All Levels |
| 5 | Schwartz Transformation with Timeframe + Price | 9.1 | Solution Aware |

### Awareness Level Rules

- **Problem Aware:** Never name the solution. Dramatize the problem. Use scenarios, timestamps, cost calculations. Make the reader feel the pain.
- **Solution Aware:** Show transformation. Before/after identity. Timeframes + pricing. Why THIS solution vs. others.
- **Product Aware:** Pure proof. Before/after numbers. Competitive benchmarks. Published data. Risk reversal.

### Universal Rules Discovered

1. **Specificity wins.** Headlines with 3+ concrete numbers consistently outscore vague headlines by 1.5+ points.
2. **Bencivenga comparison structure is the GOAT.** Side-by-side comparisons with a single differentiating variable scored 9.0+ in 85% of cases.
3. **Humor kickers add memorability without sacrificing credibility** -- but only as the LAST element, never the first.
4. **Parenthetical asides boost curiosity** by +0.5-1.0 points on average. They create an intimate, conversational tone.
5. **Data transparency promises** ("we published the data," "full report") boost curiosity by creating an irresistible content hook.
