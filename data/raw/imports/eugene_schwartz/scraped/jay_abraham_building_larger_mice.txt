GENIUS, PASSION, AND BUILDING LARGER MICE
by Brian Kurtz (published on Jay Abraham's site)

A celebration of Eugene Schwartz, legendary copywriter and author of Breakthrough Advertising.

== CORE PHILOSOPHY ==

Schwartz believed marketing wasn't about creating demand but rather "channeling and directing mass desire." His famous principle: focus on "building larger mice" and intensifying market awareness of problems and solutions.

== STRATEGIC INSIGHTS ==

PLAYING THE LONG GAME:
Rather than charging high fees, Schwartz traded his copywriting talent for mailing lists. He exchanged copy with Boardroom and Rodale for access to their health book buyer lists -- a partnership that generated millions in sales for all parties and ultimately earned him far more than direct fees would have.

MARKET UNDERSTANDING:
Schwartz spent significant time studying culture through diverse sources, including The National Enquirer, believing marketers must constantly understand where their market exists today.

PASSION OVER TACTICS:
Success requires genuine commitment. Schwartz applied intensity to everything -- including art collecting with his wife Barbara, whom he credited with keeping him connected to aesthetic excellence and possibility.

== LEGACY MESSAGE ==

His core principle: "The creativity is in your market and in your product and all you are doing is joining the two together." This foundational idea shaped decades of marketing strategy and remains relevant because human behavior remains fundamentally unchanged.
