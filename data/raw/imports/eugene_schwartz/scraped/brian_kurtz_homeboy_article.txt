EUGENE SCHWARTZ IS MY HOMEBOY
by Brian Kurtz

Brian Kurtz shares his personal relationship with Eugene "Gene" Schwartz, the legendary direct response copywriter.

== WHO WAS EUGENE SCHWARTZ ==

Kurtz describes Schwartz as "one of the most successful and prolific direct response copywriters who ever lived." His classic book, Breakthrough Advertising (1966), is considered foundational for understanding copywriting and human behavior.

== THE BOARDROOM/RODALE PARTNERSHIP ==

Rather than charging fees, Schwartz exchanged his copywriting talent for access to Boardroom and Rodale's mailing lists -- the asset his smaller company "Instant Improvement" needed most. This arrangement generated "millions of books sold" and created "the most powerful group of allies."

== CORE PHILOSOPHY ==

Kurtz emphasizes that Schwartz's decision prioritized partnership over immediate profit: "He traded his talent for the asset he needed most at the time...and money was a by-product, not the starting point."

== THE RECIPROCATION PRINCIPLE ==

The article concludes with Dr. Robert Cialdini's insight: "Reciprocation is about you, then me, then you, then me...and be the first to give service, information, concessions."

Kurtz argues this approach to partnership "leads to exponential growth and true business building...which beats a series of 'revenue events' by a mile."

== KEY TAKEAWAY ==

Schwartz's genius wasn't just in writing copy -- it was in structuring business relationships where his copywriting talent was leveraged for strategic assets (mailing lists) rather than just cash payments. This created a multiplier effect that benefited all parties exponentially.
