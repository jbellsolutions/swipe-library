Eugene Schwartz is my Homeboy
By Brian Kurtz
Published: April 27, 2016

Brian Kurtz shares his personal relationship with Eugene "Gene" Schwartz, one of history's most influential direct response copywriters. Kurtz received a coffee mug featuring Schwartz and David Ogilvy, which inspired him to revisit and update an earlier blog post about the legendary marketer.

Who was Eugene Schwartz:
- A prolific direct response copywriter and Renaissance man
- Author of Breakthrough Advertising (1966), considered "the most important book ever written on copy and creative"
- The book remains timeless and was re-published by Kurtz as a "Boardroom Book" after a copy sold for $950 on eBay

The Business Model:

Schwartz operated "Instant Improvement," publishing health books using direct mail. Rather than charging Boardroom and Rodale Books (major publishers) for copywriting services, he exchanged his writing talent for access to their valuable customer lists. This created a powerful three-way alliance that resulted in millions of books sold.

The Core Lesson:

The arrangement exemplified reciprocation over immediate profit. As Kurtz notes, "It's not always about the money." Schwartz prioritized strategic partnerships and assets he needed most rather than maximizing fees.

Famous Headlines by Schwartz for Boardroom:
- "How To Rub Your Stomach Away"
- "The Tao of Sexology"
- "How To Get The Guts Of 370 Business Magazines In 30 Minutes"

Key Facts:
- Schwartz wrote the ad that started Boardroom Reports with Martin Edelson, who came to him with only $3,500
- Boardroom grew to annual sales of more than $100 million
- Schwartz's copy was 70% Edelson's own conversation, rearranged into advertising copy
- Schwartz charged $2,500 as a copy fee (later his fees grew dramatically, with Rodale once paying $54,000 for four hours of work)

Kurtz credits this relationship with teaching him essential lessons about true business partnership and long-term value creation. After Schwartz's death in 1995, Kurtz made a deal with Schwartz's wife Barbara for exclusive rights to republish Breakthrough Advertising.
