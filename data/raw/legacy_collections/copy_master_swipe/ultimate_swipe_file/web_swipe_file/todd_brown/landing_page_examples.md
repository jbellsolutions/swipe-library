# Todd Brown Landing Page Examples and Philosophy

Todd Brown, a recognized authority in profitable customer acquisition campaigns, emphasizes simplicity, a strong "Big Idea," and a deep understanding of the prospect's psychology in his approach to landing pages and sales funnels.

## Key principles and examples of Todd Brown's landing page philosophy include:

*   **Simplicity is Key:** Brown advocates for extremely simple landing pages. One successful example cited featured an all-white background, no logo, and no header. It included a compelling headline (e.g., "Steal our number one secret that doubles your webinar profits!"), a subhead, a mention of "Free video training," and a clear call-to-action button like "Watch now," often with urgency-driven copy such as "Hurry. Limited time offer." This minimalist design aims to reduce distractions and focus the visitor on the core message and offer.
*   **Tailoring for Referred Traffic:** When traffic comes from a joint venture (JV) partner, Brown suggests cloning the landing page and customizing it to acknowledge the referrer. This might involve adding copy like "For [Partner's Name] subscribers" and potentially including the partner's picture and a positive quote about Brown or his product. This approach leverages existing trust between the referrer and their audience.
*   **The "Big Idea":** At the foundation of any successful marketing campaign, including landing pages, is a "Big Idea." This is a unique, compelling concept that captures the market's attention and differentiates the offer. The Big Idea should be both "emotionally stimulating" and "intellectually interesting."
*   **Focus on Prospect's State:** Effective landing pages address the prospect's current state, whether it's a pain point, a discomfort, a disappointment, or an unfulfilled desire. The marketing message should aim to move the prospect from their current state to a desired future state, creating demand for the product or service before explicitly selling it.
*   **Message Over Tool:** Brown stresses that the underlying message and idea are far more important than the specific tools or platforms used to build the landing page.
